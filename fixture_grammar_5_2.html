Resumen gramatical 5-2: Saber and conocer

Key Points

  • Both saber and conocer mean to know, but they are not interchangeable.
  • Both verbs have irregular forms in the first person singular yo forms. The other forms follow regular -er conjugations.
  • Use the personal a when conocer is followed by a person or pet.

Conoces a mi compañero de cuarto.

You know my roommate.

Conocemos al perro de Felipe.

We know Felipe's dog.

To Know: Saber vs. Conocer

Category Saber Conocer
Uses
  • To know facts and things
  • To know how to do something, a skill
  • To be familiar or acquainted with people, places or things
Forms

yo sé

tú sabes

él/ella; Ud. sabe

nosotros/nosotras sabemos

vosotros/vosotras sabéis

ellos/ellas; Uds. saben

yo conozco

tú conoces

él/ella; Ud. conoce

nosotros/nosotras conocemos

vosotros/vosotras conocéis

ellos/ellas; Uds. conocen

Examples

Examples with Saber

Fact:

Yo no sé la respuesta.

I don't know the answer.

Thing:

¿Sabes tú el número de teléfono?

Do you know the phone number?

Skill:

Ella sabe tocar el piano.

She knows how to play the piano.

Examples with Conocer

Familiarity or acquainted with people:

¿Conoces tú a la Sra. García?

Do you know Ms. García?

Familiarity or acquainted with place:

Yo conozco bien Buenos Aires.

I'm familiar with Buenos Aires.

Familiarity with things:

Él conoce el libro Don Quijote.

He's familiar with the book Don Quixote.
